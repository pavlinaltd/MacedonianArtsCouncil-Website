This Is My Country

The book is a rare and authentic glimpse into the immigrant life of railroad workers from the Balkans who laid tracks for the Great Northern Railroad in the early decades of the twentieth century. The novel was found in FDR's personal library in Warm Springs, Georgia, at the time of his passing.

My American Pilgrimage

Stoyan Christowe was a journalist, author, and politician. Born in Ottoman Macedonia in 1898, he immigrated to America in 1911 and came of age in the Great Plains, working on the railroad. He was a respected critic, lover of the English language and later in life, a Vermont senator.
